Bio

Yoko Nagafuchi, originally from Tokyo, Japan, began her piano studies at the age of four. While living in California, she took lessons with Lorna Griffitt at the University of California, Irvine, and received coaching from Dranoff gold medalist Shinichiro Kato in Tokyo. She participated in the Young Pianists’ Beethoven Competition all four years of high school, and in 2017, performed in the winners' masterclass. She has also appeared as a soloist with the Orange County Symphony Orchestra. An active chamber musician, her trio was selected to perform at Carnegie Weill Hall in 2016. Yoko is in her fourth year at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, where she is pursuing a dual degree in Piano Performance with Logan Skelton and Data Science. During the past summers she has participated in programs at the Aspen Music Festival and School and the Bowdoin International Music Festival, studying with Anton Nel, Alan Chow, and Kate Soyeon Lee. In master classes, she has worked with Menahem Pressler, Ronald Brautigam, Hie-Yon Choi, Jura Margulis, and Zhang Zhuo.
